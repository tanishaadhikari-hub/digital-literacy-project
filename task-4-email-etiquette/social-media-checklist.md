# Social Media Do’s

• Privacy Settings: Regularly review and use privacy settings to control who can see your personal information and posts.

• Fact-Checking: Verify the authenticity of news or information before sharing it to prevent the spread of misinformation.

• Professionalism: Maintain a professional profile picture and bio on platforms where faculty or future employers might see you.

• Giving Credit: Always attribute original work, images, or ideas to their rightful owners when resharing.

• Positive Footprint: Use your platform to share helpful resources, academic achievements, and constructive content.

# Social Media Don’ts

• Sensitive Data: Never post photos of your college ID, admit cards, or hall tickets, as these contain sensitive personal data.

• Online Conflict: Avoid engaging with "trolls" or participating in heated online arguments that could damage your digital reputation.

• Cyberbullying: Do not post, like, or share content that harasses, demeans, or targets others.

• Real-Time Location: Avoid sharing your exact live location or daily routine publicly for personal safety.

• Informal Language: Do not use slang, "text-speak," or overly informal language when communicating with professional or academic contacts.
