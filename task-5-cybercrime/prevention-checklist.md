1. UPI PIN Privacy: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money; a PIN is only required to send or authorize a payment.

2. Verify the Source: Before scanning a QR code for a "refund," verify the caller's identity through official customer care channels.

3. Use Strong MFA: Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on all social media and email accounts to prevent unauthorized access.

4. Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking: Never conduct financial transactions or log into sensitive accounts while connected to open, public Wi-Fi networks.

5. Check URL Authenticity: Always look for "https://" and the padlock icon in your browser before entering login credentials.

6. Secure Your Hardware: Set a strong biometric lock or alphanumeric passcode on your mobile device to protect your digital wallets.

7. Limit App Permissions: Regularly audit your mobile apps and revoke unnecessary permissions like access to your contacts, SMS, or camera.

8. Report Immediately: If you are a victim of a scam, immediately report it to the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the Helpline 1930
